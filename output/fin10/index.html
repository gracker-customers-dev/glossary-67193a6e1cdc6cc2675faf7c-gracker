FIN10

A financially motivated cybercrime group known for targeting various industries.

Description

FIN10 is a cybercriminal group that is primarily motivated by financial gain. This group is known for its sophisticated techniques and has been linked to various cyber attacks that focus on stealing sensitive information, such as payment card data and personal identifiable information (PII). They often leverage phishing tactics, malware, and other forms of social engineering to compromise their targets. FIN10 has been observed to operate across multiple sectors, including retail, hospitality, and healthcare, often exploiting vulnerabilities in third-party vendors to gain access to larger networks. Their operations typically involve extensive reconnaissance to identify valuable data and the potential for financial exploitation. The group's activities have raised significant concerns among cybersecurity professionals, leading to increased vigilance and the development of targeted defenses against such financial threats. Overall, FIN10 exemplifies the growing trend of organized cybercrime focused on economic exploitation, posing a serious risk to businesses and consumers alike.

Examples

  • Attacked retail chains to steal credit card information from customers.
  • Targeted healthcare organizations to access patient records for financial fraud.

Additional Information

  • Often employs ransomware as part of their extortion tactics.
  • Utilizes dark web forums to sell stolen data and services.
