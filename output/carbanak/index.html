Carbanak

Carbanak is a sophisticated cybercrime group known for targeting financial institutions using advanced malware and social engineering techniques.

Description

Carbanak is a notorious cybercriminal gang that emerged around 2013, primarily targeting banks and financial institutions worldwide. The group is believed to have originated from Eastern Europe and is known for its highly organized operations. Carbanak utilizes various techniques, including spear-phishing emails and advanced malware, to gain unauthorized access to financial networks. Once inside, they deploy the Carbanak malware, which allows them to monitor network activity, steal funds directly from accounts, and manipulate transactions to siphon money away from the institutions. The group's operations have resulted in losses amounting to hundreds of millions of dollars. The sophistication of their attacks, including the ability to remain undetected for extended periods, highlights the need for robust cybersecurity measures within the financial sector. Carbanak's tactics have influenced other cybercriminals and have led to an evolution in the methods used in cyberattacks against financial systems globally.

Examples

  • Targeted several banks in multiple countries, resulting in significant financial losses.
  • Used spear-phishing emails to infiltrate banking networks, often impersonating trusted contacts.

Additional Information

  • The name 'Carbanak' is derived from the malware used by the group.
  • Carbanak has been linked to various other cybercrime operations and has evolved into a broader network of cybercriminals.
