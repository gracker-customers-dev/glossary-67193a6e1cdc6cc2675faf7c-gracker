Deep Panda

Deep Panda is a sophisticated cyber threat actor group known for its advanced persistent threat (APT) activities.

Description

Deep Panda, also referred to as APT10 or Stone Panda, is believed to be a state-sponsored hacking group that primarily targets organizations in the technology, telecommunications, and healthcare sectors. This group is known for its highly organized and strategic approach to cyber espionage, exploiting vulnerabilities in software and using sophisticated tactics to gain unauthorized access to sensitive data. Deep Panda employs a range of techniques, including spear-phishing, malware deployment, and credential theft, to infiltrate networks and steal intellectual property and confidential information. Their operations are often characterized by long-term infiltration strategies, allowing them to remain undetected while they gather intelligence. The group is known for targeting both private companies and government entities, primarily in the United States and Asia, making it a significant player in the realm of cyber threats. Understanding the tactics and tools used by Deep Panda is crucial for organizations looking to bolster their cybersecurity measures and protect against potential breaches.

Examples

  • Targeted attacks against major technology firms to steal trade secrets.
  • Exploitation of zero-day vulnerabilities to infiltrate government networks.

Additional Information

  • Often associated with campaigns linked to Chinese state interests.
  • Known for using sophisticated malware such as PlugX and Poison Ivy.
