CopyKittens

A term used to describe a group of cyber threat actors that replicate or imitate the techniques and tactics of more established groups.

Description

CopyKittens refers to a category of cyber threat actors that adopt and mimic the strategies, tools, and behaviors of more prominent hacking groups. These actors often lack the original capabilities or resources of the groups they copy, but they leverage publicly available information, leaked tools, or media coverage of attacks to inform their activities. This phenomenon is observed in various cybercrime domains, including ransomware, espionage, and hacktivism. The CopyKittens may target less secure organizations or exploit lower-hanging fruit to achieve their goals, which can include financial gain, data theft, or political motivations. By copying the methods of established threat groups, they can evade detection, as their tactics may appear similar to well-known actors. This trend highlights the evolving nature of the cyber threat landscape, where even less sophisticated actors can cause significant harm by leveraging the tactics of their more advanced counterparts.

Examples

  • CopyKittens imitating advanced persistent threat (APT) groups to conduct espionage.
  • Emerging ransomware groups that replicate tactics of well-known ransomware families.

Additional Information

  • CopyKittens often rely on open-source intelligence and public disclosures to inform their attacks.
  • The rise of CopyKittens challenges cybersecurity defenses, as they can exploit the reputation of established groups for cover.
