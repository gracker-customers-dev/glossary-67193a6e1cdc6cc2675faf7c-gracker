APT38

APT38 is a state-sponsored cyber threat group associated with North Korea, known for its cybercriminal activities targeting financial institutions.

Description

APT38, also referred to as 'Lazarus Group,' is a cyber threat actor believed to be linked to the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). This group is particularly notorious for its sophisticated cyber-attacks aimed at stealing funds from banks and financial organizations around the world, with the intent of supporting the North Korean regime. APT38 employs a variety of tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs), including the use of malware, social engineering, and advanced persistent threat (APT) strategies to infiltrate networks and execute financial theft operations. Their activities have included attacks on the SWIFT financial messaging system and significant heists from financial institutions in various countries. The group has also been implicated in attacks against cryptocurrency exchanges, highlighting their adaptability to evolving financial environments. Given their backing by a nation-state, APT38's operations are often well-resourced and strategically calculated, making them one of the more formidable threat actors in the cyber landscape.

Examples

  • The 2016 Bangladesh Bank heist, where APT38 stole $81 million using compromised SWIFT credentials.
  • The 2020 attack on the cryptocurrency exchange, where millions in digital assets were stolen.

Additional Information

  • APT38 is part of a larger framework of North Korean cyber operations, which includes other groups like the Lazarus Group.
  • Their operations often serve to fund the regime's nuclear and missile programs amid international sanctions.
