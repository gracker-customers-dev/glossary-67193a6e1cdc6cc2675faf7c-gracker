APT10

APT10 is a Chinese cyber espionage group known for targeting global companies and government organizations.

Description

APT10, also known as Stone Panda or MenuPass, is a sophisticated cyber threat actor linked to the Chinese government, primarily engaged in cyber espionage activities. The group has been active since at least 2009 and is known for its extensive targeting of various sectors, including technology, healthcare, finance, and government. APT10's operations typically involve advanced persistent threats (APTs) that leverage social engineering, spear-phishing, and various malware to penetrate networks and steal sensitive information. One of their notable campaigns is Operation Cloud Hopper, which specifically targeted managed service providers to gain access to their clients' networks. APT10 is recognized for its use of custom malware, including the well-known PlugX remote access Trojan, which allows them to maintain persistent access to compromised systems. The group's activities highlight the ongoing threat posed by state-sponsored actors to both private and public sectors globally, emphasizing the need for robust cybersecurity measures and threat intelligence sharing.

Examples

  • Targeted major technology companies to steal intellectual property.
  • Conducted Operation Cloud Hopper to infiltrate managed service providers.

Additional Information

  • Linked to the Chinese Ministry of State Security (MSS).
  • Uses a variety of tools, including custom malware and traditional hacking methods.
