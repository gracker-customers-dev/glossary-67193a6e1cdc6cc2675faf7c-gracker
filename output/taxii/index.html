TAXII

Trusted Automated eXchange of Indicator Information

Description

TAXII, which stands for Trusted Automated eXchange of Indicator Information, is a protocol designed to facilitate the exchange of cyber threat intelligence (CTI) between organizations. It allows for the sharing of indicators of compromise (IOCs), such as URLs, IP addresses, file hashes, and other data that can help organizations detect and respond to threats more effectively. TAXII operates over HTTP and provides a framework for automated sharing, enabling organizations to receive timely updates about emerging threats in real-time. The protocol is designed to work in conjunction with the Structured Threat Information Expression (STIX), which is a language for describing threat information. Together, TAXII and STIX support a standardized approach to sharing threat intelligence, making it easier for organizations to collaborate and improve their security posture against cyber threats. This facilitates greater situational awareness and enhances the ability to proactively defend against attacks.

Examples

  • TAXII can be used by a government agency to share threat intelligence with various private sector organizations.
  • A cybersecurity vendor may implement TAXII to provide its customers with automated threat feeds.

Additional Information

  • TAXII supports different communication methods, including push and pull mechanisms for sharing intelligence.
  • It is widely adopted within the cybersecurity community and is often integrated into security information and event management (SIEM) systems.
