private sector offensive actors (PSOAs)

Individuals or groups in the private sector engaging in offensive cyber operations for various purposes.

Description

Private sector offensive actors (PSOAs) are organizations or individuals that operate in the private sector and engage in offensive cyber activities. These activities may include hacking, penetration testing, and other forms of cyber operations that are typically intended to identify vulnerabilities in systems or disrupt adversarial entities. PSOAs often act on behalf of corporate clients, government agencies, or for their own interests, which may include intelligence gathering, competitive advantage, or direct intervention in cyber conflicts. The rise of PSOAs has been facilitated by advancements in technology and the increasing complexity of cyber threats. Unlike traditional state-sponsored actors, PSOAs operate within the legal frameworks of their jurisdictions, and their methods may include the use of commercial tools and services designed for offensive security. Their activities raise ethical questions and concerns about accountability, legality, and the potential for escalation in cyber conflicts.

Examples

  • A cybersecurity firm hired to conduct penetration testing on a corporate network.
  • A private intelligence company offering services to gather information on competitors through cyber means.

Additional Information

  • PSOAs may sometimes collaborate with law enforcement or government agencies.
  • Their operations can blur the lines between legal and illegal activities in cyberspace.
