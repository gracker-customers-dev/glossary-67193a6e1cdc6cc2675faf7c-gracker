spear phishing

A targeted attempt to steal sensitive information from a specific individual or organization through deceptive emails or messages.

Description

Spear phishing is a form of phishing attack that is highly personalized and directed at a specific individual or organization. Unlike generic phishing attacks that are sent to a large number of people, spear phishing campaigns are crafted with the intent of deceiving a particular target by leveraging information that makes the communication appear legitimate. Attackers often research their targets to gather personal details, such as names, job titles, and interests, allowing them to create convincing messages that may come from trusted sources. The goal of spear phishing is usually to trick the target into revealing confidential information, such as login credentials, financial information, or to install malware by clicking on malicious links or attachments. This type of attack poses a significant risk to organizations as it can lead to data breaches, financial loss, and reputational damage. Threat intelligence tools and platforms play a critical role in detecting and mitigating spear phishing threats by analyzing patterns, recognizing malicious indicators, and training users to identify suspicious communications.

Examples

  • An email that appears to come from a company's CEO asking an employee to transfer funds urgently.
  • A message that looks like it is from a trusted vendor requesting sensitive information about the company's operations.

Additional Information

  • Spear phishing is often used in business email compromise (BEC) schemes.
  • Training and awareness programs can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim to spear phishing attacks.
