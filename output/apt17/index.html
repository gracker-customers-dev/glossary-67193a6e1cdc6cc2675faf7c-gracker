APT17

APT17 is a designated advanced persistent threat group known for cyber espionage activities.

Description

APT17, also known as Deep Panda, KungFu Kittens, and other names, is a cyber espionage group believed to be operating out of China. The group targets various sectors, including government, technology, and media, with a particular focus on organizations in the Asia-Pacific region. APT17 is known for its sophisticated tactics, including spear-phishing, use of malware, and exploitation of zero-day vulnerabilities. The group's operations often involve the use of custom-built malware and backdoors to maintain persistence within target networks. The objectives of APT17 typically revolve around gathering intelligence and stealing sensitive information, which can be leveraged for strategic advantage in political and economic contexts. The group is also noted for its ability to adapt and evolve its techniques in response to detection and remediation efforts, making it a persistent threat to organizations worldwide.

Examples

  • Targeted the Tibetan government in exile using spear-phishing emails.
  • Utilized malware like 'Deep Panda' to exfiltrate sensitive information from targets.

Additional Information

  • APT17 has been linked to campaigns against multiple sectors, including telecommunications and healthcare.
  • The group's activity has been observed since at least 2013, indicating a long-term operational history.
