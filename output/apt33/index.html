APT33

APT33 is a state-sponsored hacking group associated with Iran.

Description

APT33, also known as 'Elfin' or 'Magnallium', is a cyber espionage group that is believed to be backed by the Iranian government. This group has been active since at least 2013 and is known for targeting organizations in various sectors such as energy, aerospace, and other industries that are strategically important to Iran. APT33 employs sophisticated malware and phishing techniques to infiltrate networks and steal sensitive information. Their operations often focus on gathering intelligence to support Iranian geopolitical objectives. The group has been linked to attacks on Saudi Arabian companies and other entities in the Middle East and beyond, utilizing tools like the 'Dorkbot' malware and various custom exploits. APT33's activities reflect a broader trend of state-sponsored cyber operations aimed at disrupting adversaries and collecting intelligence through cyber means. The group’s tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) continue to evolve, making them a significant concern for cybersecurity professionals dealing with threats from nation-state actors.

Examples

  • Targeted Saudi Arabian aviation and energy sectors in 2017.
  • Utilized malware such as 'Dorkbot' for network intrusions.

Additional Information

  • APT33 is part of a larger community of Iranian cyber threat actors.
  • Their operations often coincide with geopolitical events involving Iran.
