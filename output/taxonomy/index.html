Taxonomy

A structured classification system used to organize and categorize threat intelligence data.

Description

In the context of Threat Intelligence Tools and Platforms, taxonomy refers to the systematic classification of threats, vulnerabilities, attacks, and related entities within a coherent framework. This classification allows security professionals to systematically organize and analyze threat data, making it easier to identify patterns, trends, and relationships between different types of threats. A well-defined taxonomy enhances the ability to share threat intelligence across organizations and improves communication regarding threats by providing a common language. It also aids in the development of more effective response strategies by enabling analysts to quickly assess the severity and implications of various threats. By categorizing threats into various levels, such as tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs), organizations can prioritize response efforts and allocate resources more effectively. The taxonomy can evolve as new threats emerge, ensuring that the classification remains relevant and comprehensive.

Examples

  • MITRE ATT&CK Framework – a widely used taxonomy for categorizing adversary tactics and techniques.
  • Cyber Kill Chain – a model that classifies the stages of a cyber attack to improve threat detection and response.

Additional Information

  • Taxonomies can vary significantly between organizations based on specific threat landscapes and operational needs.
  • Implementing a consistent taxonomy can facilitate better collaboration and information sharing among cybersecurity teams and across different sectors.
